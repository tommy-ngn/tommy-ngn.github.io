AI-Native PM

Most PMs use AI like a search engine with better grammar. Copy-paste a question, get a generic answer, move on. That's not AI-native. That's AI-adjacent.

The Problem

The gap between PMs who use AI and PMs who are AI-native is the same gap that existed between PMs who "used data" (looked at a dashboard once a week) and PMs who were data-driven (built instrumentation, ran experiments, made decisions from cohort analysis). The tools existed for both groups. The thinking was different.

AI-native means the AI has context. It knows your product, your users, your constraints, your decision history. It doesn't start from zero every conversation. It challenges your assumptions because it has enough context to know when you're contradicting last week's decision. It runs structured analysis — not because you told it to follow a template, but because you've built skills that encode how you think at your best.

What This Will Cover

  • Architecture: How to structure Claude (or any LLM) as a persistent thinking partner — project memory, knowledge indexing, skill orchestration. The full system, not a prompt tip.
  • Knowledge Design: What to put in context, how to organize it, what to keep out. The difference between dumping documents and designing knowledge architecture.
  • Skill Building: How to encode your best thinking into reusable AI skills — so the AI operates at your peak, not your average.
  • Workflow Integration: Where AI fits in the actual PM workflow: discovery, spec writing, stakeholder communication, competitive analysis, sprint planning. Not theoretical. From production.

This isn't a tutorial on prompting. It's the full architecture behind using AI as a PM multiplier — from someone running it in production at a company processing millions of transactions daily.
