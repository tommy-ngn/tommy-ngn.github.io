Business Acumen

Most PMs can tell you what to build. Few can tell you why it makes money. The layer above product that separates Senior PMs from the ones who get promoted.

The Problem

Product sense answers "what should we build?" Business acumen answers "why does this create value for the business, and how do we capture it?" Most PMs are trained on the first question and exposed to the second by accident, usually when a finance partner or exec pushes back on a roadmap with "but what's the business case?"

The gap shows up in promotion panels. The PM who ships features gets to Senior. The PM who can explain how those features change unit economics, defend a pricing decision, or articulate why one investment compounds and another doesn't; that PM gets to Principal.

What This Will Cover

  • Business model design: How the product creates value, how the business captures it, and why those two things are often in tension.
  • Competitive strategy: Moats, positioning, and why "we have more features" is not a strategy.
  • Unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback period, not as finance concepts, but as product levers. Which product decisions move which numbers.
  • Resource allocation: How to think about trade-offs when the roadmap has 10 things and capacity for 3. The framework behind "why this and not that."
  • Revenue architecture: Pricing, packaging, and monetization. The PM's role in the business model, not just the product model.
